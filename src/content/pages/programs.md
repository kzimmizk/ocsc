OCSC offers soccer for every age and stage, from a first introduction to the
game to competitive travel soccer. Here's what to expect at each level.

## PreK/K

OCSC's program for PreK and Kindergarten players is all about introducing the
youngest athletes to soccer in a fun, low-pressure setting right here in
Ridgway. Players practice twice a week at the Solar Ranch fields, focusing on
basic skills, coordination, and teamwork through age-appropriate games and
activities. There are no formal games at this level — just two weekly practices
designed to build confidence and a love for the sport, all close to home. This
program is a great fit for families looking for an easy, local introduction to
soccer for their youngest players.

## Academy (1st–6th Grade)

OCSC's Academy offers a fun, low-pressure introduction to soccer right here in
Ridgway, for players in first through sixth grade. Players practice twice a week
on weeknights at the Solar Ranch fields, building skills and making friends
close to home. Games are played in Montrose at McNeil Fields as part of the
Montrose Recreation District League, either Thursday evenings or Saturdays
during the day. Families should plan for the short drive to Montrose on game
days, with practices staying local in Ridgway throughout the week. This program
is a great fit for players looking to learn the game, stay active, and enjoy
soccer in a friendly, community-focused environment.

## Mountain Region League (U12 & U14)

The Mountain Region League is a step up in competition from the Academy,
offering a more advanced level of play for players ready to take their soccer
further. Through the Colorado Soccer Association, your player will compete
against teams from across the Western Slope — clubs like Montrose, Telluride,
Crested Butte, Glenwood Springs, Aspen, and Grand Junction. It's a great
opportunity for kids to experience a higher level of competition and soccer
outside Ouray County, often for the first time. As you register, please keep in
mind: away games will require travel, typically on Saturdays, with most trips
being day trips. Some weekends may include two or more games, particularly when
travel to a more distant club makes a single round trip less practical — so
please be prepared for the possibility of a fuller weekend schedule. Because
opponents and locations aren't finalized until all Mountain Region clubs
register their teams, we won't know the full season schedule or exact travel
distances until closer to the start of the season. We know travel is a real
commitment for families, and we'll share schedules as early as possible so you
can plan ahead. If you have questions about what to expect, reach out to us
before registering.
